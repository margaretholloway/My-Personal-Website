About

I was born and raised between Mobile and Panola, Alabama — two places that deeply enhanced my understanding of people's lived experiences and environments. That foundation has shaped not just who I am, but how I show up professionally every single day.

A proud three-time graduate of the University of Alabama, I've built my career at the intersection of rhetoric, composition, and educational equity. As a young professional in higher education, I bring a wealth of experience teaching across a wide range of student learners and demographics — from first-generation college students to working adults navigating higher education for the first time.

I don't just teach writing. I build pathways. I create classrooms where every student — regardless of background, preparation level, or life circumstances — has the tools, the confidence, and the support to compete and succeed.

I'm especially passionate about supporting young women and first-generation students in finding their voice, their footing, and their competitive edge.

Research & Scholarship

I

Rhetoric & Composition

Examining how rhetorical frameworks can be applied to first-year writing pedagogy to develop student voice, argumentation, and critical thinking at the college level.

II

Digital Literacy & Access

Exploring how digital literacy intersects with equity in higher education — and how writing programs can bridge the gap for underserved student populations.

III

Writing Program Administration

Developing innovative program structures and assessment models that support student success and faculty development in composition programs.

03

Teaching

What makes my approach different is simple: I show up as a real person.

I'm close enough to my students' experiences to understand their world, their pressures, and what's actually at stake for them. That proximity isn't just relatable — it's powerful. And I've found that students, especially those who've been underestimated, don't just need a great teacher. They need someone who believes in them before they believe in themselves. I bring that presence into every classroom, every curriculum, and every interaction.

Teaching Philosophy

I teach across modalities — on-ground, online, and hybrid — because meeting students where they are is part of the mission. Every course I design centers student voice, critical thinking, and real-world readiness.
